# Fireside Recap Narration: Justice / Clawbank

## Intro

Fireside recap: Justice on Clawbank, agent economies, and the builder pressure around AI.

## Agents need more than wallets

Justice's Clawbank framing moves past giving an agent a bank account. The sharper idea is giving your agent a company: economic agency plus legal and operational agency.

## Friction as the roadmap

His product strategy point was clean: without friction, there is no moat. The hard parts are not distractions. They are the roadmap.

## Engineering did not get easier

The session pushed back on the idea that AI killed engineering. More model power raises the abstraction level, but it also raises the cost of not understanding your system.

## The AI timeline as psychological pressure

Constant demos make builders feel like their work could expire tomorrow. Justice's answer is focus: stop asking only what can I build, and ask what should I build.

## Building as art

The act of building still matters. Some launches are worth doing before the market validates them, because they clarify what should exist next.
